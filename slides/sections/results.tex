\section{Results}

\begin{frame}{Results \& Demo}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item We built the full project: trained models, report, dashboard, and slides.
    \item The main result is simple: the adaptive looped model beat both LLM baselines.
    \item It also ran much faster because it stopped early on easy cases.
    \item We packaged a dashboard demo so the predictions can be shown live.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Main Result on C-MAPSS FD001}
  \begin{tabular}{lcccc}
    \toprule
    Model & Accuracy & Macro F1 & Avg. Depth & Latency \\
    \midrule
    Baseline & 0.9746 & 0.8609 & 0.00 & n/a \\
    Fixed loop & 0.9877 & 0.8891 & 6.00 & n/a \\
    Adaptive loop & 0.9838 & 0.9183 & 1.21 & 0.17 ms \\
    Frozen LLM & 0.9136 & 0.3183 & 0.00 & 16.95 ms \\
    Task-adapted LLM & 0.9098 & 0.3454 & 0.00 & 32.11 ms \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\vspace{0.4cm}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Best overall balance came from the adaptive loop.
    \item It used about 80\% less looping than the fixed-depth version.
    \item It was about 189x faster per sample than the task-adapted LLM.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{What These Numbers Mean}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item The looped model was not just faster. It was also better at separating the health states.
    \item The LLM baselines struggled much more on warning and failure cases.
    \item Most remaining mistakes were between \texttt{normal} and \texttt{warning}, not total failure misses.
    \item That matters because maintenance decisions depend on catching the early warning zone.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}
